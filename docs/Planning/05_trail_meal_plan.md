## **Allergy Watch Summary**

**Major allergens present in the 2026 trail menu:**

| Allergen | Present in |
| ----- | ----- |
| Wheat / Gluten | Widely present — crackers, pretzels, pasta dishes, cereals, most cookies. GF options include some waffle items and some snack bars. |
| Milk / Dairy | Widely present — most dinner entrées, cheese cups, granola bars, hot cocoa. |
| Soy | Present in several items — some granola bars, some crackers, Ramen broth. |
| Tree Nuts (Almonds, Cashews) | Multiple bars and trail mix items — RXBAR, Kodiak bars, Snickerdoodle bars, Almond Sweet & Salty bar. |
| Peanuts | Peanut butter items in lunches, some trail mix. |
| Eggs | RXBAR Blueberry (Egg Whites). Some shared-facility items may contain egg traces. |
| Fish / Shellfish | Tuna in multiple lunch items. |

**Cross-contamination warnings:**

Multiple items are produced on shared equipment with peanuts, tree nuts, milk, wheat, soy, or sesame. The Guidebook states: "Philmont trail food is by necessity a high-carbohydrate, high-caloric diet. It contains wheat, milk products, sugar and corn syrup, and artificial coloring/flavoring."\[^20\]

## **Allergy and Substitute Food Planning Checklist**

Anyone with a food allergy must verify the full ingredients against the official 2026 Philmont Trail Menu and Ingredients document. This report is a planning summary, not a substitute for reading the actual label.

**Required steps for anyone with a food allergy or dietary restriction:**

* \[ \] Download and read the full 2026 Philmont Trail Food Menu and Ingredients PDF.  
* \[ \] Compare your allergy or restriction against every meal you will eat.  
* \[ \] Identify which items in each meal you cannot eat.  
* \[ \] Source substitute food at home and bring it to Philmont.  
* \[ \] Package substitute food by meal. Label each bag with your expedition number, name, and the meal it replaces (example: "Lunch 5 — \[Name\] — Expedition 12-23").  
* \[ \] Bring substitute food bags to Logistics on Day 1 of the official trek.  
* \[ \] At backcountry food pickups, both standard bags and your substitute bags will be available.  
* \[ \] You are responsible for removing items you cannot eat from standard issue bags.

The Guidebook states: "Food substitutions may be made ONLY for medical (including allergies), religious, or vegetarian/vegan reasons. All food shipped to the backcountry is subject to inspection to ensure that food is being substituted for these reasons."\[^21\]

For vegetarian or vegan crew members, most entrees contain meat. Meat items include chicken (jerky, pouched chicken), beef (jerky, ramen), pork (snack sticks, Spam), and pepperoni. Substitute sources must be brought from home.

For religious dietary restrictions (Kosher, Halal, or other), contact Philmont directly at 575-376-2281 or Philmont.Camping@scouting.org before arrival to confirm what substitution options are available.

**Anyone with a severe allergy involving anaphylaxis must:**

* Bring an EpiPen (two is recommended) in date.  
* Have the EpiPen accessible during all activities, not buried in a pack.  
* Brief the crew and advisors on the allergy, symptoms, and how to use the EpiPen.  
* Confirm medication presence and expiration at medical recheck.
